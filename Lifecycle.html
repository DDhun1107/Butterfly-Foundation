The Lifecycle of Butterflies

Table of Contents

Egg Caterpillar Pupa Butterfly Migration

Butterflies are quite remarkable in terms of their lifecycle.

    They aren't like many other animals that either go through the process of emerging out of their egg, or being birthed by their parent. They undergo both!

  1. Egg

    2.     They first go through the gestation period inside an egg laid by their parent on a leaf or a flower.

        The eggs are usually laid during the summer, spring, or fall seasons. Butterflies usually do not lay eggs during the winter to avoid the egg from being damaged from wind or just the harsh cold conditions.

        Once the egg is laid, the gestation period, that is, the time it takes for the egg to be ready to hatch is approximately four days. And after that, a tiny caterpillar will come out of the egg!

  2. Caterpillar

    3.     Caterpillars generally look like worms, but with legs. Most of the time, they have six legs, along with prolegs, that are limbs that also look like legs. They help caterpillars move around and attach to surfaces around themselves.

        The sole purpose of caterpillars is to eat. That's what they do during the day, and also during the night. This is the reason as to why they get big so fast. Their diet is mostly leaves, which is why when farmers see caterpillars, they immediately go for a round of pesticides. While taking care of their crops is also important, the survival of caterpillars is also quintessential.

    Caterpillars usually live for one to three weeks.

  3. Pupa

    4.     After its time as a caterpillar, the insect settles down on a leaf or flower. It either secretes a silk like substance around itself and builds a cocoon, or its body develops a hard shell under its skin and the caterpillar sheds to reveal the ready-made cocoon. The cocoon is also known as the pupa.

        In it, the caterpillar experiences many changes, one of them is their physical appearence. They develop features such as large wings and a small body.

        The caterpillar's residence in the pupa may range from a few weeks to even a few years! Thus, this is the reasons why during their previous stage, that is the caterpillar stage, their only job is to eat and store as much energy as possible.

  4. Butterfly

    5.     When the time arrives for our new buddy to come out of the pupa, it either eats it open, or the pupa breaks open as it moves.

        There emerges the butterfly, slightly moist. During the next few hours, the butterfly will dry out and extend its wings for the first time.

        Then, instinctively, it takes flight in search for flowers, mainly to feed on their nectar. Unlike its previous self, it cannot feed on leaves, so it has a only nectar diet and it loves it!

        The main purpose of butterflies is to reproduce, and having wings means that they can fly to an appropriate location to lay eggs.

        The reason why butterflies matters so much is because they are important pollinators. Without them, plants would not be able to produce fruits which eventually would prevent them from reproducing. Thus, the lack of butterflies in our ecology would lead to a huge downfall.

  • Migration

    •     As winter approaches, butterflies from the northern hemisphere fly to the south. This ensures that they have a consistent food source, and that they can escape the cold weather.

        Butterflies not having a circulatory system means their body temperature solely depends on the surrounding environment. And thus, when the temperature dips too much, they have trouble flying and when its too hot, they will overheat and perish.
