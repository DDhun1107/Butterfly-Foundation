Butterflies of the World

    You want to see some butterflies? We're here to help, as long as you don't hurt them! Our database contains information about what types of butterflies you might find around you.

Europe

Europe has a lot of exotic looking butterflies. So if you're looking to travel to see butterflies, that's the place to go! We promise you won't regret it.
Camberwell Beauty Clouded Yellow Oleander Hawk Moth Long Tailed Blue Death Head Hawk Moth
Africa

Africa also has its fair share of butterflies. Even though one would think that the temperature would be too high, the butterfly concentration is mainly down in the Tropic of Capricorn, where the temperature is most ideal for butterfly survival.
Swallowtail African Migrant Axiocerses Harpax Black Spot Bematistes Acraea
Asia

Asia is also an amazing place to travel to for butterfly enthusiasts. Here are some butterflies you may encounter there.
Swallowtail Birdwing The Great Morman Brush Footed The Chocolate Pansy
North America

North America is home to many butterfly species. The number is an estimated 563 species! Although during the winter, most of them migrate south. So if you're trying to see some butterflies, try visiting from June to September.
Blues and Hairstreaks Brush-Footed Skippers Swallowtails Metalmarks
South America

Central South America has a high temperature, which makes it less likely to have a lot of butterflies. It does have some though. And because of migration from the north, they have similar species as those in North America. They can more easily be seen in southern summer months.
Brush-Footed Skippers Swallowtails Metalmarks Clearbacks
Oceania

Also being partly in the tropics, Australia has a large variety of butterflies too!
Clearwing Swallowtail Blue Triangle Tailed Emperor White Tiger Monarch


If you want to get more information about the location of butterflies, check out our resources page. It shows you where we got our data, and where to find a whole lot more!
